Notes on Operational Semantics

1. Introduction

State $\sigma$ is a set which is defined by a partial function $f$ that is from the set of variable $Var$ to the set of values $Num$ such that: \begin{array}{ll} S \subset \{(v, n) \mid v \in Var , n \in Num\}, \\ \sigma = \{(x,y) \in S \mid \forall x.\exists y \neg \exists y'(f(x) = y \wedge f(x) = y' \wedge y' \neq y)\}. \end{array}

Evaluation context of Aritmetic Expressions

\begin{equation} E_A ::= [] \mid E_A + a \mid n + E_A \mid E_A \times a \mid a \times E_a \end{equation} where $[]$ is the hole that express the location of the target arithmetic expression, $a$ is the meta variable for the arithmetic expressions (Isn't it instruction??). $E_A[a]$ denotes the arithmetic expression $E_A$ which is replaced with the hole by $a$.

Instruction

\begin{equation} I_A := X \mid n_1 + n_2 \mid n_1 \times n_2 \end{equation}

Reduction Rules

\begin{array}{lcllr} (E_A[X], \sigma) &\longrightarrow & (E_A[\sigma(X)], \sigma) & & \text{(A-VAR)}\\ (E_A[n_1 + n_2], \sigma) &\longrightarrow& (E_A[n], \sigma) &\text{(n is the sum of $n_1$ and $n_2$)} & \text{(A-PLUS)}\\ (E_A[n_1 \times n_2], \sigma) &\longrightarrow& (E_A[n], \sigma) &\text{(n is the product of $n_1$ and $n_2$)} & \text{(A-MULT)} \end{array}
