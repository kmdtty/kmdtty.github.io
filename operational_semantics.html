Notes on Operational Semantics

1. Introduction

Operational Semantics is a way to define the meaning of Languages. There are several ways to give the meanging of languages such as:

Denotational Semantics
Define the meaning by mathmatical structures.
Axiomatic Semantics (Hoare Logic)
Define the meaning by the precondition and postcondition of the language with the logical proof system.
Operational Semantics
Define the meaning by the executing steps in an abstruct machine.

\begin{equation} a ::= n \mid X \mid a_0 + a_1 \, \mid a_0 \times a_1 \end{equation} where $a, a_0, a_1$ are the meta variables of arithmetic expression, $n$ is the metavaiable of integers, $X$ is the meta variable of Variables.
State $\sigma$ is the set which is defined by the partial function $f: Var \rightharpoonup Num$ such that: \begin{array}{ll} S \subset Var \times Num, \\ (\_,y') \in S,\\ \sigma = \{(x,y) \in S \mid \forall x.\exists y \neg \exists y' (f(x) = y \wedge f(x) = y' \wedge y' \neq y) \}. \end{array} $\sigma[x]$ denotes the value of $f(x)$.

Evaluation context of Aritmetic Expressions

\begin{equation} E_A ::= [] \mid E_A + a \mid n + E_A \mid E_A \times a \mid a \times E_a \end{equation} where $[]$ is the hole that express the location of the target arithmetic expression, $E_A[a]$ denotes an arithmetic expression which is replaced with the hole by $a$.

Any arithmetic expressions except $n$ can be expressed by $E_A[a]$. (Proof is needed)

Instruction

\begin{equation} I_A := X \mid n_1 + n_2 \mid n_1 \times n_2 \end{equation}

Instruction is an subset of arithmetic expression that is evaluated at the hole of an Evaluation context.

Reduction Rules

\begin{array}{lcllr} (E_A[X], \sigma) &\longrightarrow & (E_A[\sigma(X)], \sigma) & & \text{(A-VAR)}\\ (E_A[n_1 + n_2], \sigma) &\longrightarrow& (E_A[n], \sigma) &\text{(n is the sum of $n_1$ and $n_2$)} & \text{(A-PLUS)}\\ (E_A[n_1 \times n_2], \sigma) &\longrightarrow& (E_A[n], \sigma) &\text{(n is the product of $n_1$ and $n_2$)} & \text{(A-MULT)} \end{array}
